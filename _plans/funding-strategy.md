# Funding Strategy: NHPL and Sabin's Post-Doc Path

**Date**: 2026-03-11
**Purpose**: Comprehensive funding roadmap for the organisation and Sabin's career

## 1. NHPL Organisational Funding

### A. Small Grants (under GBP 50k) -- Apply Now

**1. ESRC Impact Acceleration Account (IAA) -- University of Strathclyde**
- **Award**: GBP 5,000--15,000
- **Duration**: 3--6 months
- **Eligibility**: Strathclyde researcher (check if PhD students can be PI or if supervisor must be PI)
- **Fit**: "Accelerating the policy impact of PhD research on Nepal's health insurance programme." The evidence portal is the impact pathway.
- **Timeline**: Rolling applications; 4--6 week turnaround
- **Action**: Talk to supervisor and Faculty IAA coordinator this week. **Single quickest win available.**

**2. Scottish Funding Council (SFC) Innovation Vouchers / Knowledge Exchange Grants**
- **Award**: GBP 5,000--25,000
- **Eligibility**: Scottish HEI + external partner. If NHPL registers in Nepal, the Nepal entity could be the partner.
- **Caveat**: Primarily designed for Scotland-based partnerships; Nepal angle may be a stretch. Worth a conversation with the KE office.

**3. British Academy / Leverhulme Small Research Grants**
- **Award**: Up to GBP 10,000
- **Eligibility**: Must hold a salaried position at a UK HEI. PhD students typically NOT eligible as PI -- supervisor would need to be PI.
- **Fit**: Fund fieldwork, workshops, or a policy engagement trip to Kathmandu.
- **Timeline**: Two rounds per year (October and March). Check britishacademy.ac.uk.

**4. GCRF -- Strathclyde Internal Allocation**
- **Award**: GBP 5,000--30,000
- **Note**: GCRF was significantly cut post-2021 ODA reduction, but some universities retain residual internal funds.
- **Action**: Ask Faculty research office whether Strathclyde still has GCRF or ODA-aligned pump-priming funds.

**5. R4D (Results for Development) Small Grants**
- **Award**: USD 10,000--30,000
- **Eligibility**: LMIC-based organisations. **Requires Nepal registration.**
- **Fit**: Health financing and UHC evidence synthesis is core to R4D's mission.

**6. IDRC (Canada) -- Successor to Think Tank Initiative**
- **Award**: CAD 20,000--50,000
- **Note**: TTI closed 2024, but IDRC continues funding policy research institutions in the Global South.
- **Eligibility**: **Requires Nepal registration.**
- **Action**: Monitor idrc.ca for "Evidence and Policy" or "Governance and Justice" calls.

**7. iHEA Early-Career Researcher Grants / Conference Bursaries**
- **Award**: USD 1,000--5,000
- **Fit**: Present NHPL's work at iHEA World Congress. Networking value exceeds the monetary value.

**8. GIZ / BACKUP Health Programme**
- **Award**: EUR 10,000--50,000
- **Eligibility**: Civil society organisations in GIZ partner countries (Nepal qualifies). **Requires Nepal registration.**
- **Fit**: Supports civil society engagement in health financing and social health protection. NHPL's NHIP focus aligns directly.

### B. Medium Grants (GBP 50k--200k) -- Build Toward Over 6--12 Months

**9. NIHR Global Health Research -- Development Awards**
- **Award**: GBP 50,000--150,000
- **Eligibility**: UK-based PI at eligible institution (Strathclyde qualifies). **Requires named LMIC partner institution** -- hence why the Nepal MoU is critical.
- **Fit**: "Building evidence infrastructure for health policy in Nepal" is precisely what NIHR Global Health funds.
- **Timeline**: Periodic calls; monitor nihr.ac.uk. Next call likely late 2026 or early 2027.
- **Strategic note**: Most natural medium-term funder for NHPL within the UK system.

**10. Wellcome Trust -- Discovery Research**
- **Award**: GBP 50,000--300,000
- **Eligibility**: Any career stage, eligible institution. Current focus: infectious disease, climate and health, mental health.
- **Fit**: Partial. Stronger if framed around mental health systems or climate-health financing in Nepal. A pure health economics proposal may not fit current Wellcome priorities.

**11. ESRC/FCDO Joint Fund for Poverty Alleviation Research**
- **Award**: GBP 100,000--500,000
- **Eligibility**: UK-based PI with LMIC co-investigators. Requires formal LMIC partner.
- **Note**: Has been restructured multiple times post-ODA cuts. Monitor ukri.org.

**12. Swiss Agency for Development and Cooperation (SDC)**
- **Award**: CHF 50,000--200,000
- **Note**: Switzerland has a significant health programme in Nepal (funds Suaahara nutrition programme, NHIP-adjacent work).
- **Action**: Approach SDC Nepal country office directly. They commission studies and may welcome a standing evidence partner.

**13. WHO Alliance for Health Policy and Systems Research (AHPSR)**
- **Award**: USD 20,000--80,000 (embedded research grants, knowledge translation grants)
- **Eligibility**: LMIC-based researchers. Some schemes require WHO country office endorsement.
- **Fit**: NHPL's evidence-to-policy translation mission aligns directly with AHPSR's mandate. The "embedded research" framing (Suresh inside HIB, Prajjwal engaging MoHP) is exactly what AHPSR values.
- **Action**: Monitor ahpsr.who.int. Reach out to WHO Nepal's Health Systems team for endorsement.

**14. USAID (via implementing partners)**
- **Note**: USAID funds through large implementing partners (Abt Associates, Palladium, RTI International) who then subcontract.
- **Action**: Identify who holds the current USAID/Nepal health systems contract. Approach about subcontracting for evidence synthesis. **Requires Nepal registration.**

## 2. Post-Doc Fellowships for Sabin

### Tier 1: Strong Fit -- Apply as Soon as Eligible

**1. ESRC Postdoctoral Fellowship** ★ HIGHEST PRIORITY
- **Value**: Salary + up to GBP 12,000 research costs, 12 months
- **Eligibility**: PhD submitted within 12 months of fellowship start. UK HEI.
- **Timeline**: Annual call, usually January/February closing
- **Fit**: Continue NHIP research, build portal, publish from dissertation. NHPL *is* the ESRC impact story.
- **Strategic note**: Probably the single best first post-doc option.

**2. British Academy Postdoctoral Fellowship** ★ CAREER-TRANSFORMING
- **Value**: 3 years salary (approx GBP 38,000--42,000/year) + GBP 6,000/year research
- **Eligibility**: Within 3 years of PhD award. Any nationality. UK HEI.
- **Timeline**: Annual call, closing October/November. ~8--10% success rate.
- **Strategic note**: The 3-year duration could transform NHPL from a side project into a real institution.

**3. Leverhulme Early Career Fellowships**
- **Value**: 3 years. 50% salary (host pays other 50%) + GBP 6,000/year research
- **Eligibility**: Within 5 years of PhD. No permanent position.
- **Timeline**: Annual call, February/March closing
- **Caveat**: 50% co-funding means Strathclyde must agree to cover half salary. Discuss with department early.

**4. NIHR Development and Skills Enhancement Award**
- **Value**: Up to GBP 150,000 over 1--3 years
- **Fit**: Build health systems research capacity with Nepal focus.

**5. Wellcome Early-Career Awards**
- **Value**: Up to GBP 400,000 over 5 years
- **Eligibility**: Within 5 years of PhD. Must align with Wellcome priorities (infectious disease, climate, mental health).
- **Fit**: Partial. Stronger if you frame around mental health economics or climate-health financing in Nepal.

### Tier 2: US-Based / International

**6. Harkness Fellowships in Health Care Policy and Practice** ★ CAREER-DEFINING
- **Funder**: The Commonwealth Fund (New York)
- **Value**: Full funding for 12 months in the US (~USD 120,000 total)
- **Eligibility**: Mid-career health policy researchers from Commonwealth countries (UK eligible)
- **Timeline**: Annual, closing September
- **Fit**: Outstanding. Health policy fellowship at Harvard/Johns Hopkins/etc. Could collaborate with Mukesh at UNC.

**7. Takemi Fellowship -- Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health**
- **Value**: Stipend + office at Harvard, 10 months (~USD 45,000--55,000)
- **Eligibility**: Mid-career health professionals from developing countries
- **Timeline**: Annual, due December/January
- **Fit**: Focuses on health policy and management in developing countries. Access to Harvard's global health network.

**8. Alexander von Humboldt International Climate Protection Fellowship**
- **Value**: EUR 2,670/month + research costs, 12--24 months in Germany
- **Fit**: Only if you develop a genuine climate-health financing research agenda.

**9. WHO/TDR Fellowships**
- **Value**: USD 30,000--50,000
- **Eligibility**: LMIC nationals (Sabin qualifies as Nepali)
- **Fit**: Primarily infectious disease/clinical. Expanding into implementation science.

### Tier 3: Longer-Shot, Build Toward

**10. UKRI Future Leaders Fellowships** -- Up to GBP 1.5M over 4 years. Needs publications + demonstrated impact. 2--3 years out.

**11. Marie Sklodowska-Curie Postdoctoral Fellowships (EU)** -- 12--24 months. Requires cross-border mobility. Could work with Swiss TPH or Bergen Centre for Ethics and Priority Setting.

### The Key Insight: Fellowship = NHPL Growth

**Every fellowship application should include NHPL as the "impact pathway."**

UK funders (ESRC, NIHR, British Academy, UKRI) all require articulating impact beyond academia. NHPL is the answer. The framing:

- **ESRC IAA**: "This funding will accelerate the policy impact of my PhD research through NHPL's evidence portal."
- **ESRC Postdoc**: "My post-doctoral research will be translated into policy-ready outputs through NHPL, a bilingual evidence-to-policy initiative I co-founded."
- **British Academy**: "The fellowship will support both original health economics research and the institutional development of NHPL as Nepal's first standing health evidence translation service."
- **NIHR**: "NHPL's evidence portal and established relationships with MoHP provide a direct pathway from research to policy influence."

Every pound spent on Sabin's fellowship also builds NHPL. They are not competing priorities -- they are the same story at different scales.

## 4. Non-Grant Revenue

### A. Commissioned Evidence Synthesis Reports (Near-Term)

**Target clients and approach:**

| Client | Day rate | Approach |
|--------|----------|----------|
| FCDO Nepal | GBP 200--500/day | Health Adviser at British Embassy, Kathmandu |
| WHO Nepal | Similar | Health Systems team |
| UNICEF Nepal | Similar | Health/nutrition section |
| SDC Nepal | Similar | Country office directly |
| GIZ Nepal | Similar | Health programme team |
| World Bank Nepal | Similar | Health sector support projects |

**How to start:** Produce one evidence brief on a topic of active interest (NHIP enrollment, BHSP costing, CHW professionalisation) and share proactively with 2--3 organisations. Demonstrate value first.

**Pricing:** USD 2,000--5,000 per brief. Do not undercharge -- international consultancies charge USD 5,000--15,000 for equivalent work.

### B. Technical Assistance Subcontracts

Large USAID/FCDO/World Bank projects subcontract to local orgs. Identify who holds current Nepal health systems contracts, approach about evidence synthesis subcontracting. **Requires Nepal registration.**

### C. Training and Workshops (Medium-Term)

- Health economics short courses for MoHP officials, HIB staff, INGO managers
- Evidence literacy workshops for parliamentary research staff
- Charge NPR 5,000--10,000 per participant, or seek WHO/UNICEF sponsorship

### D. Models from Comparable Orgs

- **CGD model**: Core funding from a few large donors, all work published openly
- **IIHMR Jaipur**: Research institute + fee-paying educational programme
- **AERC model**: Multi-donor pooled fund supporting researcher network
- **WHO E2P platforms**: Standing evidence-to-policy mechanisms embedded in national health systems -- NHPL could position as Nepal's E2P platform

### E. Newsletter Monetisation (Later)

Not now. Build to 500+ subscribers first. Then use the subscriber list as evidence of reach in grant applications: "NHPL's monthly digest reaches 300 health policymakers in Nepal" is a powerful line in a funding proposal.
